🩺 Medical Insurance Cost Prediction

This project builds a predictive model to estimate individual medical insurance charges based on demographic and lifestyle attributes using Linear Regression. It also includes data-driven insights and business recommendations.

📌 Objective

  • Analyze key drivers of medical insurance costs
  • Build a regression model to predict charges
  • Provide actionable insights for insurance pricing strategy

🧩 Dataset Overview

Sourced from Kaggle: Medical Cost Personal Dataset

FeatureDescription
ageAge of insured individual
sexGender of policyholder
bmiBody Mass Index
childrenNumber of dependents
smokerSmoking status
regionResidential region in the US
chargesBilled medical charges

🔍 Exploratory Analysis

  • Distribution charts for age, charges, children, BMI
  • Pie charts for categorical features (sex, smoker, region)
  • BMI categorized into health levels
  • 3D bar plot for average cost by age group and smoking status

⚙️ Data Preparation

  • Outlier removal using Z-score
  • One-hot encoding for categorical features
  • Feature scaling with StandardScaler
  • Engineered feature: age * charges for interaction

🤖 Modeling – Linear Regression

  • R² Score: 0.92
  • RMSE: 0.28
  • The model performs well without overfitting

🔍 Feature Importance

  • Smoking status is the top cost driver
  • Obesity and number of children are secondary drivers
  • Age and region also influence cost moderately

💡 Business Insights

  • Target high-risk groups (smokers, obese individuals)
  • Use pricing tiers for risk-based premiums
  • Launch preventive programs to reduce future payouts

📦 Files
